# Size the system around priority loads

When people say they want a solar system, they usually mean they want stable power for a few essential appliances. That is why the first step is not the panel count. The first step is deciding what must stay on when grid power drops.

## List only what matters

- Lighting circuits
- TV and decoder
- Router
- Fans
- Refrigerator, if required

Write down the estimated wattage and expected hours of use for each item. That gives you a better basis for inverter and battery sizing than vague descriptions like "full house backup."

## What usually causes bad sizing

- assuming all appliances run at the same time
- underestimating startup surge for motors
- ignoring battery depth of discharge
- planning around perfect sunshine every day

## A practical rule

Build around your most important loads first. If the budget allows, scale the system in stages instead of buying a too-small setup that immediately disappoints.
