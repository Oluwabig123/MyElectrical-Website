# Start with the load, not the accessories

Most wiring problems begin when people add new accessories without checking the circuit behind them. A clean-looking finish does not mean the cable size, breaker rating, or connection quality is correct.

## Inspect these areas first

- Confirm the **breaker size** matches the circuit use.
- Check whether the **existing cable size** can carry the added load.
- Look for overheated accessories, darkened terminals, or loose faceplates.
- Confirm all new routing will stay dry, protected, and accessible for maintenance.

## Common warning signs

If you already notice flickering lights, warm switches, or breakers tripping during heavy use, treat that as a wiring review issue first. Adding more load on top of an unstable circuit usually makes the fault worse.

> Safe expansion starts with assessment, not guessing.

## A simple upgrade sequence

1. Review the existing circuit purpose.
2. Measure or estimate the new connected load.
3. Verify breaker, cable, and accessory ratings.
4. Plan clean routing before cutting walls or ceilings.
5. Test the completed circuit under real load.
