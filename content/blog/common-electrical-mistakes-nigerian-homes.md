# Small shortcuts become expensive faults

Many home electrical issues start with quick fixes that were never reviewed against real usage patterns.

## Mistake one: adding appliances without checking circuit load

A circuit designed for light usage can fail early when heavy appliances are added gradually without upgrades.

## Mistake two: mixing incorrect breaker and cable combinations

Oversized breakers can fail to protect undersized cables. Protection and conductor sizing should be matched.

## Mistake three: poor termination quality

Loose or overheated terminals are common causes of unstable lights, hot switches, and recurring trips.

## Mistake four: ignoring early warning signs

Frequent flicker, warm accessories, and repeated nuisance trips are warning signals, not normal behavior.

## Better approach

Treat upgrades as a mini design task: review load, route, cable, protection, and accessory quality together.
