# Lighting should be planned as a system

Clients often focus on the fixture style first, but the final result depends more on spacing, beam direction, and control zones than on the fitting price alone.

## Mistake one: even spacing without purpose

Even spacing sounds safe, but it can flatten a room and leave the real focal areas underlit. Seating, artwork, kitchen worktops, and circulation paths do not all need the same treatment.

## Mistake two: one switch for everything

Layered spaces need layered control. Ambient, accent, and task lighting should not always be tied to one switch point.

## Mistake three: planning too late

Once POP work is finished, your best routing and placement options are already limited. Resolve the layout while access is still open.

## A better workflow

1. Define what the room is used for.
2. Mark focal points and working zones.
3. Choose fixture types for each role.
4. Separate switch groups before installation.
