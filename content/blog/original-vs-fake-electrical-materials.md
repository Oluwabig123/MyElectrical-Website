# Fake materials usually fail at the worst time

Many clients only discover fake or low-grade components after faults begin: overheated accessories, unstable breakers, premature light failure, or repeated maintenance costs.

## Check packaging and batch consistency

- Compare print quality, spelling, and seal quality.
- Check that codes, ratings, and batch markings are consistent.
- Avoid mixed units with mismatched labels in the same carton.

## Verify ratings against your actual load

A product can be original and still be wrong for your job. Confirm current ratings, voltage class, and usage environment before buying.

## Buy from accountable channels

Prioritize trusted distributors and keep receipts, batch photos, and packaging records. Traceability matters when faults occur later.

## Use installation review, not just product confidence

Even genuine materials fail if installed poorly. Pair material checks with proper routing, protection setup, and final testing.
