# Oduzz Cables and Wires Guide

Cable choice is one of the most important electrical decisions in any project. Oduzz recommends cable categories based on safe current carrying needs, installation route, and environmental exposure.

## Typical use context

- Lighting circuits.
- Socket and appliance circuits.
- AC and high-load dedicated lines.
- Inverter/solar interconnections.
- Outdoor or conduit-protected runs.

## Good practice highlights

- Use cable sizes that match actual load and run length.
- Separate lighting and power circuits where possible.
- Protect cables using suitable conduit and routing discipline.
- Confirm terminations are tight and professionally finished.

## Risk of poor cable choices

Undersized cables can cause overheating, voltage drop, nuisance trips, and fire risk. Counterfeit products may have poor conductor quality and insulation failure under normal load.

## Oduzz policy on cable recommendations

Final cable gauge and route recommendations are confirmed after understanding full load details and site layout. For safety and performance, avoid guessing cable sizes for high-load equipment without professional assessment.

## What to share for accurate support

Provide appliance list, breaker ratings, route distance estimates, and project location. Photos of existing panel/cable entries help Oduzz advise faster.
